Checklist for Your Pet's Full Body Health

You probably see your pet every day. And he may look perfectly fine to you, but is he really? What does a normal, healthy pet look like?

Ears

  • Clean; no bad odor, redness, or excessive wax
  • Regularly check for signs of infection or parasites
  • Maintain regular cleaning routine

Eyes

  • Bright and clear; no redness or cloudiness; not watery
  • Watch for signs of discomfort such as squinting or rubbing
  • Regular vet check-ups to monitor eye health

Mouth

  • No “doggy breath,” red or swollen gums, or discolored teeth
  • Regular dental cleanings and check-ups
  • Provide dental chews or toys to help maintain oral health

Skin & Coat

  • Healthy-looking coat; no red, flaky, or excessively dry skin; no lumps or scabs
  • Regular grooming and bathing
  • Check for fleas, ticks, and other parasites

Bones & Joints

  • Normal activity level; no limping or trouble standing up; no hesitating to walk or use stairs
  • Ensure a balanced diet to support joint health
  • Provide regular exercise appropriate for your pet's age and breed

Weight

  • A visible “waist” or slight tuck-in behind the ribs; ribs should be easy to feel but not see
  • Monitor weight regularly and adjust diet as necessary
  • Consult with your vet for a suitable weight management plan

Heart & Lungs

  • No coughing, sneezing, wheezing, or labored breathing; watch for reluctance to exercise or play; or getting tired or winded more easily than normal
  • Regular vet check-ups to monitor heart and lung health
  • Provide a smoke-free environment

Digestive System

  • Normal appetite; no diarrhea or vomiting; watch for swollen abdomen, burping more than usual, passing gas, passing stool that’s a different color, having trouble passing stool
  • Provide a balanced diet with proper nutrients
  • Ensure access to clean, fresh water at all times

Urinary System

  • No accidents in the house (if house-trained); watch for urine that looks or smells different, trouble urinating, or inability to urinate (if your pet is trying to urinate and can’t, seek immediate medical attention. It could indicate a life-threatening blockage!)
  • Regular vet check-ups to monitor urinary health
  • Provide plenty of opportunities for outdoor breaks

Your healthy pet begins with you! Scheduling regular wellness visits with your veterinarian, keeping your pet's vaccines up-to-date, staying on top of parasite prevention, feeding a 100% nutritionally complete diet that is formulated for your pet, and getting adequate exercise can work wonders for your pet's health.

Stay informed about the latest happenings in the world of pets.

New Outbreak of Canine Influenza in California

Date: July 3, 2024

A recent outbreak of canine influenza has been reported in several counties in California. Pet owners are advised to monitor their dogs for symptoms such as coughing, sneezing, and lethargy.

Source: California Department of Public Health

Impact: Hundreds of dogs affected, multiple veterinary clinics overwhelmed.

Prevention Tips

  • Keep your dog away from crowded places
  • Ensure vaccinations are up to date
  • Consult your vet if symptoms appear

Feline Parvovirus Outbreak in New York City

Date: June 28, 2024

Veterinarians in New York City have reported an outbreak of feline parvovirus, also known as feline panleukopenia. The virus is highly contagious and can be fatal for kittens and unvaccinated cats.

Source: NYC Animal Care Centers

Impact: Several shelters are experiencing high infection rates.

Prevention Tips

  • Ensure your cat is vaccinated
  • Keep your cat indoors to reduce exposure
  • Disinfect any areas where an infected cat has been

Rabies Alert in Texas

Date: June 20, 2024

A rabies alert has been issued in Texas following several confirmed cases in wildlife. Pet owners are advised to ensure their pets are vaccinated against rabies and to avoid contact with wild animals.

Source: Texas Department of State Health Services

Impact: Increased vigilance in affected areas.

Prevention Tips

  • Vaccinate your pets against rabies
  • Avoid contact with wild animals
  • Report any unusual animal behavior to local authorities

Pet Food Recall Due to Contamination

Date: July 1, 2024

A major pet food manufacturer has issued a recall for several of its products due to potential contamination with Salmonella. Pet owners are urged to check the recall list and avoid feeding the affected products to their pets.

Source: FDA

Impact: Nationwide recall affecting thousands of products.

Prevention Tips

  • Check the recall list for affected products
  • Dispose of any contaminated pet food
  • Contact your vet if your pet shows signs of illness

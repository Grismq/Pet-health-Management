Common Pet Symptoms to Watch Out

Fever
  • Increased Body Temperature: Use a pet thermometer to check if your pet's temperature is above the normal range (101-102.5°F for dogs and cats).
  • Lethargy: Your pet may seem unusually tired and less active than normal.
  • Shivering or Panting: Signs of discomfort such as shivering in cold environments or panting in warm environments can indicate a fever.
Ulcers
  • Visible Sores: Look for open sores or lesions in the mouth, skin, or other body parts.
  • Difficulty Eating: Your pet may have trouble eating or show signs of pain while chewing.
  • Excessive Drooling: Increased salivation can be a sign of ulcers, particularly in the mouth.
Facial Swelling
  • Swollen Areas: Noticeable puffiness or swelling around the eyes, muzzle, or neck.
  • Pain or Discomfort: Your pet may show signs of pain when the swollen area is touched.
  • Difficulty Breathing: Severe swelling can lead to breathing difficulties, which is a medical emergency.
Short Term Lethargy
  • Low Energy Levels: Your pet may appear unusually tired and less enthusiastic about activities they typically enjoy.
  • Reduced Appetite: A lack of interest in food or treats can accompany lethargy.
  • Sleeping More: An increase in sleeping time, even during the day, can indicate lethargy.
Swollen Lymph Nodes
  • Enlarged Nodes: Feel for swollen lymph nodes under the jaw, behind the knees, or in the shoulder area.
  • Discomfort: Your pet may show signs of discomfort or pain when these areas are touched.
  • Lethargy: Accompanying signs of illness such as lethargy or fever may also be present.
Diarrhea with Mucus
  • Loose Stools: Frequent, loose, or watery stools with a gelatinous mucus coating.
  • Straining: Your pet may strain excessively while defecating.
  • Dehydration: Signs of dehydration such as dry gums and loss of skin elasticity can accompany diarrhea.
Hair Loss
  • Bald Patches: Noticeable areas of missing fur on the body.
  • Excessive Shedding: Increased fur loss, more than usual, found around the house or on your pet's bedding.
  • Itching or Scratching: Frequent scratching, biting, or licking the affected areas.
Jaundice
  • Yellowing of Skin or Eyes: A yellow tint in the whites of the eyes, gums, or skin.
  • Dark Urine: Urine that is darker than usual can be a sign of jaundice.
  • Lethargy and Loss of Appetite: General signs of illness such as being less active and eating less.
Dehydration
  • Dry Gums and Nose: Check for dryness in the mouth and nose.
  • Skin Elasticity: Gently pinch the skin on the back of your pet’s neck. If it doesn't snap back quickly, your pet may be dehydrated.
  • Sunken Eyes: Eyes may appear sunken and dull.
Seizures
  • Convulsions: Uncontrolled shaking, twitching, or jerking of the body.
  • Loss of Consciousness: Your pet may collapse and become unresponsive.
  • Foaming at the Mouth: Excessive drooling or foaming can occur during a seizure.
Hyperesthesia
  • Excessive Sensitivity: Overreaction to touch, such as flinching or twitching when petted.
  • Dilated Pupils: Eyes may appear wide and pupils larger than usual.
  • Self-Mutilation: Your pet may excessively groom or bite themselves.
